About Victoria Falls

Welcome to Victoria Falls, a natural wonder of the world, and one of the most popular tourist destinations in Africa. Sacredly located on the Zambezi River, on the border between Zambia and Zimbabwe with falls 1.7 kilometers (1 mile) wide and 108 meters (360 feet) high, making them the largest waterfall in the world by volume of water.

The Mosi-oa-Tunya, which means "The Smoke that Thunders." This is a fitting name, as the falls create a huge cloud of mist that can be seen from miles away. The noise of the falls is also deafening, and can be heard up to 40 kilometers away.

There are many ways to experience Victoria Falls. You can walk along the edge of the falls, take a boat trip on the Zambezi River, or go on a helicopter tour. There are also a number of adventure activities available, such as bungee jumping, white-water rafting, and skydiving. No matter how you choose to experience Victoria Falls, you are sure to be amazed by its beauty and power. This is a truly unforgettable place that should be on everyone's bucket list.
